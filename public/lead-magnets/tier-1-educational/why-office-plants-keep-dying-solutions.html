üíÄ The Deadly Pattern

  • ‚Ä¢ Watering on a calendar schedule

üíö The Life-Saving Fix
  • ‚Ä¢ Designate ONE person as primary caretaker
  • ‚Ä¢ Use the "finger test" - stick finger 2 inches into soil
  • ‚Ä¢ Water only when soil is dry at depth
  • ‚Ä¢ Ensure ALL pots have drainage holes
  • ‚Ä¢ Create plant-specific watering schedules
Emergency Warning Signs of Overwatering
  • Yellow leaves starting from the bottom: Classic overwatering symptom
  • Musty, sour smell from soil: Root rot has begun
  • Leaves dropping despite wet soil: Roots are suffocating
  • Soft, black stems: Plant is beyond saving
  • Fungus gnats flying around: Soil is staying too wet
Nick's Professional Solution: The 3-2-1 Watering System
3 fingers deep: Check soil moisture 3 inches down, not just the surface
2 people maximum: Never allow more than 2 people to be responsible for one plant
1 drainage check: Water should flow out the bottom within 1 minute of watering
Emergency Overwatering Recovery Protocol
  1. Stop all watering immediately - Even if soil looks dry on top
  2. Remove plant from pot - Inspect roots for black, mushy areas
  3. Trim dead roots - Cut with sterile scissors until you see white, healthy roots
  4. Repot in fresh, well-draining soil - Never reuse waterlogged soil
  5. Place in bright, indirect light - Helps plant recover faster
  6. Wait 1-2 weeks before watering - Let roots recover from trauma
Nick's 15-Year Observation:
In 15 years of office plant consulting, I've found that the most caring employees kill the most plants. Overwatering kills more office plants than all other factors combined. The solution isn't less care - it's smarter care.
